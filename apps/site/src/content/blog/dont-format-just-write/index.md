> Draft.

Give someone a rich text editor and watch what happens before they write anything: they pick a heading. They bold something. They make a list, then a nested list, then they fix the indentation on the nested list. Twenty minutes later there's a beautifully formatted document containing almost no thought.

Formatting _feels_ like work because it produces something that looks finished. But it's the most seductive form of procrastination there is — it's busywork that leaves a polished artifact, so you don't even notice you avoided the actual writing.

### The blank page already won

Every formatting decision is a tiny off-ramp from the thing you sat down to do. Heading or no heading? Bullets or paragraph? Each one is a moment where your attention leaves the _idea_ and goes to the _container_. By the time you've made the container nice, the idea has cooled.

### Plain text is a feature

When the only thing you can do is type words, the only thing you can do is think. A little markdown — a `#title` here, a `#hashtag` there — is enough to stay findable later without becoming a layout project now. Everything past that is decoration you'll never reread.

### Capture beats polish

The honest, ugly, five-second line is worth more than the styled paragraph, because it actually exists and it actually says what happened. You can always tidy a thought you captured. You can't tidy one that evaporated while you were choosing a font.
