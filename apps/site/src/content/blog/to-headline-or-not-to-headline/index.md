> Draft.

A title is a small commitment. Before you've written the entry you have to decide what it's _about_ — and naming a thing you haven't written yet is its own little stall. So SostaLog makes the `# title` optional, which raises a fair question: when is a headline worth it, and when is it just friction in a hat?

### When a headline earns its place

A title is really a **handle** — something to grab the entry by later. It earns its place when:

- The entry is one you'll deliberately come back to (a decision, a milestone, a turning point).
- It's the kind of thing you'd search for by name, not by hashtag.
- Skimming the timeline, you'd want this one to announce itself.

In those cases the title isn't decoration. It's the difference between findable and lost.

### When to skip it

Most lines don't need one. _"Coffee. Brain fried."_ does not want a headline; a headline would be heavier than the thought. The timestamp and the first few words already tell you everything. Forcing a title onto a throwaway line is exactly the formatting-as-procrastination trap — you stall naming something that didn't need a name.

### A rule of thumb (for the revision)

Write the line first. If, having written it, an obvious title is already sitting there — use it. If you have to _think_ of one, you don't need one. The headline should fall out of the entry, never block it.
